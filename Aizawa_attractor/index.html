The Aizawa Attractor

The Aizawa Attractor is a type of strange attractor that arises from a set of nonlinear differential equations. It was introduced by K. Aizawa and is known for its complex and beautiful chaotic behavior. The attractor can be visualized in three-dimensional space, revealing intricate patterns that are sensitive to initial conditions.

\[ \begin{align*} \frac{dx}{dt} &= (z - b) x - d y \\ \frac{dy}{dt} &= d x + (z - b) y \\ \frac{dz}{dt} &= c + a z - \frac{z^3}{3} - (x^2 + y^2) (1 + e z) + f z x^3 \end{align*} \]

Typical parameter values that result in chaotic behavior are:

  • \( a = 0.95 \)
  • \( b = 0.7 \)
  • \( c = 0.6 \)
  • \( d = 3.5 \)
  • \( e = 0.25 \)
  • \( f = 0.1 \)
